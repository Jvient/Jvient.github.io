I am a **IA Oceanogaphy Scientist** at [SHOM](https://www.shom.fr/) (Service Hydrographique et Océanographique de la Marine), where I work as an Innovation Lab agent at the interface between **physical oceanography** and **artificial intelligence**.

My work focuses on developing AI-driven methods to analyze, reconstruct and forecast ocean variables — turbidity, surface dynamics, water masses — by combining in-situ observations, satellite remote sensing and hydrodynamic simulations. I collaborate with operational teams at SHOM and academic partners (IMT Atlantic, IUEM, Ifremer) to design oceanographic products, proofs-of-concept and applied research projects.

I hold a **PhD in Ocean Data Science** from the Université de Bretagne Occidentale (2019–2022), conducted jointly with [IMT Atlantic](https://www.imt-atlantique.fr/) and SHOM under the supervision of Christophe Delacourt, Ronan Fablet and Frédéric Jourdin. My thesis explored how **deep learning** and **data assimilation** can bridge satellite ocean colour observations and hydrodynamic-sedimentary models to better reconstruct the spatio-temporal dynamics of coastal turbidity.

I am a graduate engineer in **Geology and Environment** from UniLaSalle, with a background combining geosciences, numerical modeling and machine learning.

## Research interests

- Deep learning for ocean and Earth observations
- Data assimilation, neural interpolation and physics-informed AI
- Ocean colour, turbidity and coastal dynamics
- Coupling of observations and hydrodynamic models
- Open-source tools for operational oceanography
